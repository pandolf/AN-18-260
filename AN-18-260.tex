\begin{abstract}
Results from the September 2018 CERN H4 test beam, where LYSO crystal bars, read out by pairs of SiPM's, were exposed to a beam of positively-charged pions with 80 GeV of energy, are discussed. The bars respresent a possible design of the Barrel Timing Detector, in the context of the upgrade of the CMS detector in view of the High Luminosity phase of the Large Hadron Collider. We show that a timing resolution better than the target of 30~ps is achieved with various crystal-SiPM configurations. \end{abstract}
